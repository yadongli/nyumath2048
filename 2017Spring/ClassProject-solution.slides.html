Background¶

You are advising a client, Ms. Rich, who owns a successful business. Ms. Rich is planning to retire in three years, she is extremely confident that she can find a suitable buyer for her business soon (definately within 3 years). Ms. Rich is interested in protecting her net worth, which is largely locked in her company, against any adverse business environment in the next 3 years. The earnings of Ms. Rich's company is mostly tied to the its three big customers, JNJ, PG and MMM. Ms. Rich believes that a equally weighted portfolio in JNJ, PG and MMM is the key risk factor of her company, if the value of that portfolio loses 50% of its current value, then Ms. Rich's company will suffer major financial loss due to lack of orders from its largest customers. Ms. Rich wants protection against such severe risk for the next 3 years.

Ms. Rich has been talking with her financial advisors at Giltman Shakes & Co., and they offered her a knock-in barrier put option, with monthly observation dates. The terms of the barrier option are:

  • Inception and exipry date: the option starts on the 1st day of the month, expires on a month end 3Y after its inception.
  • Reference basket: consists of three stocks of [JNJ, PG, MMM] with equal dollar weights at the trade inception
  • Observation dates: every month end before the option expiration, including the final maturity date (which is on a month end).
  • Trigger event: the basket value is below 50% of its starting value on any of the observation dates. It does not trigger if the basket value dips below 50% betweewn the observation dates, but comes back above the 50% barrier on the next observation date.
  • Payoff: if triggered, Giltman Shakes will pay $ \left(\$10,000,000.00 \times \frac{s(0) - s(3Y)}{s(0)}\right)^+$ at option maturity (i.e., t = 3Y), where $s(t)$ is the value of the basket at time $t$. Essentially, when the barrier is breached, the instrument turns into an European put with \$10MM notional amount.
  • The option expires worthless at t=3Y if the barrier is not breached on any of the observation dates.
  • Ms. Rich cannot manage collateral as her company is not a financial institution, therefore neither party posts or receives any collateral during the life time of the trade.

Ms. Rich likes the proposed trade, and she wants your expert opinions. She does have some concerns: Even though Giltman Shakes is a reputable firm, it had gone to the brink of bankrupcy during the financial crisis. Ms. Rich is rightfully worried about Giltman Shakes' ability to pay. In case if Giltman Shakes is in default and the reference basket breaches the 50% barrier, Ms. Rich is expected to receive only 40% of the promised payoff through the bankrupcy litigations. Ms. Rich has raised this concern to Giltman Shakes, the traders in Giltman Shakes offered her a 2% discount from the option's fair value, which is the valuation without taking into account any counterparty default risk. Giltman Shakes' trader claims it is a generous offer because the firm currently has a single A credit rating, and the historical default probability of single A firms over 3Y is only 0.5%, and the expected recovery of Giltman Shacks is 40%.

Modeling tasks¶

There are several required modeling steps in this projects:

  1. Build a CDS curve for Giltman Shakes, and compute its market implied default probability term structure.
  2. Price the fair value (without the counterparty risks) of the barrier option using Monte Carlo simulation. Please feel free to make your own assumptions, or use available historical data to estimate necessary parameters.
  3. Apply one variance reduction technique, you get more credits for more savings in computational time. There can be more than one good choices, use your creativity.
  4. Compute the value of the barrier option with the default risk of Giltman Shakes, and advise Ms. Rich whether she should take the 2% discount offer.

The following are optional steps that will earn you extra credit:

  1. Propose any alternative structures that might serve Ms. Rich's needs better, you may win over her business from the Giltman Shakes,
  2. Risk and hedging, if you were the dealer from Giltman Shakes who takes the opposite side of the trade, what are the main risk factors and how would you hedge this barrier option.

Hints:

  • If you are considering control variate, PDE could be a useful way to value your control variate.
  • For step 4, you'd need to simulate Giltman Shake's default time, the simplest way is to consider Giltman Shakes has defaulted if its stock price goes below a threshold, which can be calibrated to the default probability. In reality, after a company defaults, its stock price goes to 0 permanently. For this project, you can assume that company could come back to life from bankruptcy, and its stock continues to follow GBM even in default. This assumption simplifies the calibration of the default threshold and the simulation of default events, you only need to check if the Giltman Shakes' stock is below the calibrated threshold at the final maturity of the barrier option, you do not need to track if Giltman Shakes has defaulted prior to the maturity date. This assumption is clearly wrong, but it does not affect the main point we are trying to capture here.
  • We have CDS implied and rating based default probabilities for Giltman Shakes, it is important to think about and explain which default probability you should use in oder to price the option and why.

import proc

def barrier_payoff(obs, thr, defaulted) :
    breached = np.any(np.less(obs, thr))
    v = 0
    if breached : #breached barrier
        v = disc[-1]*np.maximum(1. - obs[-1], 0.)
        if (defaulted) :
            v *= recovery
            
    return v

obs_freq = 12
samples_per_period = 10
dt = 1./(obs_freq*samples_per_period)
obs_idx = np.arange(samples_per_period, tm*obs_freq*samples_per_period+1, samples_per_period) - 1

npath = 5000
ts = np.arange(dt, tm+1e-10, dt)
disc = np.exp(-r*ts)

mgbm = proc.MF_GBM(np.ones(nf)*r, cov)

x0 = np.ones(nf)
defprob = 1. - np.exp(-tsit0(ts))

es = np.random.normal(size=[npath, len(ts), nf])
paths = mgbm.draw(es, x0, dt)

gs = paths[:, :, -1]
def_thr = np.array([np.percentile(s, 100.*d) for s, d in zip(gs.T, defprob)])
sim_dp = np.array([1.*np.sum(np.less(s, d)) for s, d in zip(gs.T, def_thr)])/npath

arith_avg = lambda x : np.mean(x[obs_idx, :-1], 1) 
geom_avg = lambda x : np.exp(np.mean(np.log(x[obs_idx, :-1]), 1))

bv = []
rv = []
cv = []
covs = []

for xs in paths :
    gs_defaulted = xs[-1, -1] < def_thr[-1]
    bv.append(barrier_payoff(arith_avg(xs), thr, False))
    rv.append(barrier_payoff(arith_avg(xs), thr, gs_defaulted))
    cv.append(barrier_payoff(geom_avg(xs), thr, False))
    
    covs.append(np.cov(np.diff(np.log(xs.T)))/dt)
    
bv = np.array(bv)
rv = np.array(rv)
cv = np.array(cv)
